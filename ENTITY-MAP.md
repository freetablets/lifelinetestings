# Entity Map

## Core relationships

- **FCC** establishes federal Lifeline policy and consumer rules.
- **USAC** administers Lifeline under FCC oversight.
- **Lifeline Support / National Verifier** verifies eligibility and provides consumer application, document, household, provider-search, and recertification guidance.
- **Lifeline** primarily supports eligible phone or internet service. It does not guarantee a tablet.
- **Medicaid** and **SNAP** are qualifying assistance programs. The programs do not issue Lifeline devices.
- **EBT** is a delivery system or card associated with benefits. An EBT card may not contain enough information to prove SNAP participation.
- **SSI**, **Federal Public Housing Assistance**, **Veterans Pension or Survivors Benefit**, and qualifying **Tribal assistance programs** are additional program-based routes.
- **Income eligibility** generally uses 135% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines and depends on economic household size and location.
- A **household** is analyzed by shared income and expenses, not only a street address.
- **Participating phone and internet companies** enroll eligible consumers, provide service, and control any device promotion, inventory, model, fees, shipping, activation, returns, and support.
- **Program participation documentation** connects an applicant to an accepted program through name, issuer, program, and date.
- **Recertification** confirms continued eligibility and may be automatic or require a response.
- **Service usage** helps maintain no-cost service and is distinct from annual eligibility review.
- A **provider device promotion** is a commercial or enrollment offer attached to provider terms, not a guaranteed federal tablet benefit.
- **ACP** is a separate historical program that ended in 2024 and is not a current device-application route.
