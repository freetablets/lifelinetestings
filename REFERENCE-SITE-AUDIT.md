# Reference Site Audit

## Scope and method

Reference domain: `governmenttabletfree.com`  
Audit date: 2026-07-12  

The homepage, Apply Now page, Medicaid guide, EBT guide, and Resources page were inspected through the available web renderer. The legal, About, Contact, blog archive, feed, robots, and sitemap endpoints could not all be fetched from the available environment. They are marked as not verified rather than guessed. The sandbox itself had no outbound DNS, so the build process did not run a conventional recursive crawler. This is an honest limitation of the reference inventory.

## Executive assessment

The reference site covers valuable search demand around Lifeline, Medicaid, SNAP/EBT, documents, providers, and applications, but the visitor journey is built around a “free tablet” promise rather than a careful explanation of the federal service discount. The most serious issue is outdated ACP framing. The homepage and Apply page call ACP paused, while the Resources page presents ACP benefits as active and combinable with Lifeline. The EBT article says some prior households can still claim the historical device discount. That creates a material accuracy and AdSense trust risk.

## Content depth and information architecture

The accessible site has a shallow top navigation, a small blog cluster, and legal links. The Medicaid and EBT articles carry most of the informational depth. Important tasks such as household analysis, status tracking, recertification, service use, denial troubleshooting, provider comparison, and scam prevention are not developed as independent authority pages.

The new site expands the architecture to 42 canonical pages, including separate pillars for eligibility, income, programs, documents, National Verifier, providers, household rules, recertification, service requirements, device promotions, scams, editorial standards, and ten supporting articles.

## Keyword targeting and search journey

The reference targets high-volume phrases directly, including free government tablet, free tablet with Medicaid, free tablet with EBT, and apply now. It does not consistently separate informational, verification, provider-selection, and transactional intent. That makes the Apply page especially risky because the form resembles an official or provider submission despite the site being an information publisher.

The replacement journey starts with a direct qualified answer, then routes visitors through eligibility, household, documents, official verification, provider comparison, service, and optional device terms.

## Internal linking

The reference repeatedly links Home, Resources, Blog, About, Contact, Apply Now, Medicaid, and EBT pages. The accessible capture shows limited contextual linking into deeper tasks because those pages do not exist. Footer repetition dominates the link graph.

The replacement uses contextual hubs and every indexable page receives multiple internal links. A generated link map records each link and verifies that the target exists.

## Visual design and conversion

The reference uses a conventional WordPress landing-page layout with a hero, eligibility block, application steps, team cards, testimonials, promotional CTA, and popups. The conversion language includes Claim Now, Get Your Free Tablet Now, and application forms. Team identities, testimonials, social icons, and promotional outcome statements are not substantiated in the accessible pages.

The replacement uses an original navy, emerald, gold, ivory editorial system, custom illustrations, source panels, tables, timelines, checklists, and a browser-only preparation pathway. It avoids testimonials, fake staff, countdowns, stock counters, ratings, seals, and application submissions.

## Technical SEO and schema

The accessible HTML shows basic page titles, headings, navigation, images, and WordPress archive conventions. Canonical and schema details were not fully exposed by the web-rendered text. Robots, sitemaps, RSS, and child sitemap files were not retrievable in the available audit environment.

The replacement includes self-referencing canonicals, unique metadata, Open Graph, WebSite, Organization, WebPage, BlogPosting, BreadcrumbList, FAQPage where visible FAQs match, sitemap, robots, feed, llms.txt, manifest, 404, headers, redirects, and a schema report.

## Accessibility, mobile, and performance

The reference exposes a skip link and menu toggle in rendered text, which are positive signals. Full keyboard, contrast, responsive, and performance testing was not possible through the available reference crawl. Popups, promotional CTAs, comment forms, and repeated footer content can increase distraction and DOM weight.

The replacement uses semantic landmarks, keyboard navigation, focus styles, accessible fields, responsive tables, reduced motion, print CSS, reserved image dimensions, local assets, no remote fonts, and lightweight vanilla JavaScript.

## E-E-A-T, AEO, and GEO

The reference publishes team names, titles, photographs, testimonials, and success claims without visible verification in the inspected pages. Primary sources are not consistently placed near changing claims. Dates appear on articles but not on the principal homepage and application claims.

The replacement uses an organization-level Editorial Team, source hierarchy, fact-check dates, next-review dates, claim logging, correction policy, provider mention policy, direct answers, complete entity relationships, and citations close to high-risk statements.

## Legal, trust, and AdSense risks

High-risk elements include:

- ACP described as paused or active after the program ended in 2024.
- A lead-like application form on an informational site.
- External redirects from Apply and popup CTAs to other “free device” domains.
- Guaranteed or near-guaranteed device, unlimited service, shipping, brand, and delivery language.
- Named staff, testimonials, and social channels without visible substantiation.
- Phrases that make the site sound like it helps applicants obtain devices rather than explaining information.

## Content opportunities

The strongest expansion opportunities are current program status, household examples, 2026 income limits, evidence quality, National Verifier troubleshooting, provider comparison, service use, recertification, denial reasons, status stages, device-offer terms, scam prevention, official-resource directories, and transparent editorial policies. Those opportunities are implemented in the new site without copying the reference structure or wording.
