# Originality Report

## Method

The new site was generated from an original information architecture, brand, design system, page specifications, official-source facts, and custom illustrations. It does not import reference HTML, CSS, images, testimonials, staff identities, calls to action, or provider claims.

A local review normalized main-content text and checked repeated 8-word sequences within the new site. Sitewide navigation, disclosures, official program names, required legal concepts, source names, and intentionally repeated safety distinctions were excluded from editorial similarity judgments.

## Reference-risk review

Reference-specific phrases such as “Claim Now,” “Get Your Free Tablet Now,” named staff, applicant testimonials, fixed shipping windows, named guaranteed device brands, unlimited-service promises, and active or paused ACP language were not used as conversion claims.

The pages use a different heading order and visitor journey: program status, role separation, household, evidence, official verification, provider comparison, service continuity, device terms, safety, sources, and editorial review.

## Internal repetition

The build uses a consistent direct-answer and source-panel pattern. Repeated factual statements are limited to safety-critical distinctions, including that Lifeline primarily supports service, device offers vary, official verification is required, and ACP ended in 2024. The calculated QA did not flag any page above the configured 15% repeated-paragraph threshold after shared site components were excluded.

## Corrections completed

- Removed guarantee-style language.
- Kept Medicaid and SNAP separate from device fulfillment.
- Treated an EBT card as potentially insufficient proof.
- Separated official eligibility from provider enrollment and shipping.
- Removed fake people, testimonials, awards, ratings, seals, partnerships, locations, and social counts.
- Used current dated income figures and review metadata.
